US-South Korea nuclear-armed submarine deal is a serious threat: Kim Yo-jong'


North Korean leader Kim Jong-un's sister, Kim Yo-jong, has warned that South Korea's recent deal with the United States could lead to a new threat. According to North Korean state media, Kim Yojong said that North Korea must strengthen its capabilities to reduce these threats.
It should be noted that the United States has not only agreed to deploy its nuclear-armed submarines in South Korea, but has also agreed to include Seoul in its strategy to counter nuclear threats from the North. In response, South Korea has agreed not to develop nuclear weapons.


The agreement, known as the Washington Declaration, came to light this week when US President Joe Biden met with his counterpart South Korean President Yoon Suk-yeol in the US. Speaking about the agreement, Kim Yo Jong added, "The more our adversaries increase their nuclear war drills and the more nuclear assets they deploy around the Korean Peninsula during the drills, the more we have the right to defend ourselves." The usage will become stronger.'.

He warned that as a result of this move, there will be more serious threats to peace and security not only in Northeast Asia but also for the world...

Kim Jong-un's sister, Kim Yo-jong, holds a high position in the Central Committee of the Workers' Party of Korea and is known as a powerful and highly influential figure like her brother. On the other hand, US President Joe Biden has praised this agreement and claimed that "this deal will strengthen the cooperation of the allies to prevent North Korea's attack."
